How to use TremorSense

TremorSense min (the current version available on the Play Store) has been designed for ease of use and simple functionality. To get the most out of TremorSense, you should:

  • Ensure you are in a stable environment. If you're in a bus, for example, every bump will be interpreted as part of your tremor, and this will skew the results. Sit down at a desk in a stationary position before using TremorSense.
  • Extend your arm in front of you, with your palms pointing towards the ground. Place the device on top of your extended hand and tap the measure button.
  • Try to keep the screen of the device pointed vertically throughout the test, as rotational movement will skew results.
  • The test should take less than 10 seconds to complete. During this time, the circular graph will give you a realtime readout of your tremor
  • The test is complete when a number appears in the center of the red graph. This number is an abstract measurement of tremor severity.

Do not attempt to balance the device on your hand if you think it will fall off. Instead, try an alternate posture. Any of the following are fine:

  • Affix the device to your wrist with a strap, and use the standard posture.
  • Arms outstretched, palm facing downwards. Grasp the phone in your hand with the screen pointing upwards. Wait for 15-20 seconds for the test to complete.
  • For Parkinsonian tremor, place the hand on your thigh
  • Any other position where the hand is unsupported and the screen of the device is pointed upwards.

Interpreting the Results

The number given when the recording is finished is an abstract indicator of tremor severity. Due to factors such as device accelerometer accuracy and tremor frequency, it is difficult to accurately compare results across subjects. This means that even though someone else has a higher score than you, it doesn't necessarily mean that their tremor is more functionally severe. TremorSense is best used to measure personal variations in tremor severity. In other words, what makes your tremor worse? what makes it better? Is it stable or worsening over the long term?

In general, tremor severity can be classified as follows:

  • 0 - 5: No significant tremor.
  • 6 - 15: Mild tremor. Some difficulty performing fine motor tasks.
  • 16 - 30: Moderate tremor. High degree of difficulty performing fine motor tasks.
  • 26 - 50: Severe tremor. Fine motor tasks virtually impossible, some tasks very difficult.
  • 51 - 100: Very severe tremor. Substantial difficulty with everyday tasks.
  • 101+: Extreme tremor. Highest level of clinical severity, with difficulty performing any function with affected body parts.

Values generated by TremorSense are not reflective of a diagnosis of essential tremor or any other condition and should not be used in a diagnostic setting. This app is for personal use only.

What is tremor?

Tremor is a bodily phenomenon whereby shaking is experienced that is outside the individual's control. This occurs in everyone, but is not clinically significant in most individuals. The result is a set of rhythmic, involuntary muscle contractions that decreases stability and steadiness of affected body parts and may cause difficulty in performing certain tasks. Everyone experiences tremor to some degree, but it is considered pathological in some individuals.

How does TremorSense work?

TremorSense utilises the accelerometer in your device to detect even the slightest movement of the hand. In a nutshell, TremorSense takes 1000 readings from the accelerometer, calculates the standard deviation of these values, and scales the result according to a modified arctangent plot. This gives a number roughly between 0 and 100 which corresponds to tremor severity.
